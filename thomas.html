Thomas J. Watson Sr.

Thomas J. Watson Sr. was the founder of IBM (International Business Machines Corporation). He was born on February 17, 1874 in Campbell, New York and died on June 19, 1956 in New York City. Watson was a businessman and served as the president of IBM from 1914 to 1956.

Career highlights:

  • Founded IBM in 1911
  • Served as the president of IBM from 1914 to 1956
  • Developed the first commercially successful tabulating machine in the early 1900s

IBM accomplishments:

Year Accomplishment
1914 Watson becomes president of IBM
